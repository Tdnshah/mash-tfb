About #TobaccoFreeBharat
Did you know what causes 50% of all cancer cases in the country directly or indirectly and 13.5 lakh deaths annually - Tobacco!
Every other day, we lose our fellow Indians to this grave addiction. Imagine the plight of a young boy Anish, 9 - who lost his father to throat cancer, and Seema, who lost her young son to tambakhu addiction.
There are many more scary and heartwrenching stories like these as more than 26.6 crore adult Indians, which is close to 25% of the country’s adult population, are consuming tobacco in some form or the other! Scary, right?
But together, we can stop young Indians from burning their lives away. We can save a million lives by strengthening the ‘Cigarettes and Other Tobacco Products Act’ – to achieve a Swasth Bharat.
As the young force of India, its upon us - to be fit and healthy and take our country to glorious heights in the Amrit Kaal. It’s time to #StrengthenCOTPANow and build a #TobaccoFreeBharat, together!

The Bharat of #AmritKaal will be a #TobaccoFreeBharat, Swasth Bharat.
#StrengthenCOTPAnow to save youth from tobacco addiction.

How can we
Strengthen
COTPA

Increase the legal age of tobacco consumption

Tobacco consumption at a young age significantly increases the risk of long-term health issues and addiction. By increasing the legal age of tobacco consumption from 18 to 21, we can discourage tobacco initiation among the youth and promote healthier lifestyle choices. This measure aims to safeguard the well-being of young individuals and create a generation less affected by tobacco-related harms.

Increase the legal age of tobacco consumption

Comprehensive ban on advertisement of tobacco products

Advertising tobacco glamorizes and normalizes smoking, making it appealing, especially to the youth. A comprehensive ban on tobacco advertising will necessarily reduce the social acceptance of tobacco use, prevent the initiation of smoking, and protect our Yuvaa populations from the hazardous effects of tobacco consumption.

Comprehensive ban on advertisement of tobacco products

Ban on sale of single cigarette sticks

The sale of single cigarette sticks is a common practice that allows youth to purchase cigarettes at a lower cost and increases the likelihood of casual smoking and experimentation. By banning the sale of single cigarette sticks, we can disrupt this practice, discourage smoking, and prevent the easy availability of tobacco products.

Ban on sale of single cigarette sticks

Increase fines for not abiding by the law

Strengthening the fines for non-compliance sends a clear message that the enforcement of anti-tobacco laws is a priority. Higher fines act as a deterrent, encouraging individuals and establishments to abide by the law and reinforcing the importance of a tobacco-free environment. This measure aims to promote compliance and ensure the effectiveness of anti-tobacco regulations.

Increase fines for not abiding by the law

Removal of Designated Smoking Areas

Designated Smoking Areas (DSAs) have long been used as a compromise to balance the rights of smokers and non-smokers. However, the presence of DSAs still exposes non-smokers to secondhand smoke and undermines efforts to create smoke-free environments. By advocating for the removal of DSAs, we can achieve a truly smoke-free Bharat, protecting the health and well-being of everyone.

Removal of Designated Smoking Areas
